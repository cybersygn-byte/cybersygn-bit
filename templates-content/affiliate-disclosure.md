## 1. What an Affiliate Relationship Means

1.1 **Definition.** An "**Affiliate Link**" is a tracked link or code that allows a
merchant or affiliate network to attribute a purchase, sign-up, or other action to
the Publisher. An "**Affiliate Program**" is the arrangement under which the
Publisher may receive a commission, referral fee, credit, or other compensation
(together, "**Affiliate Compensation**") for those actions.

1.2 **How it works.** When you click an Affiliate Link and then complete a
qualifying action with the merchant, the merchant or network may pay the Publisher
Affiliate Compensation. This typically occurs at no additional cost to you; the
price you pay is generally the same whether or not you use an Affiliate Link.

1.3 **No additional cost.** Except where expressly stated otherwise, using an
Affiliate Link does not increase the price you pay. Where an exclusive discount is
offered through an Affiliate Link, that will be stated in the relevant content.

## 3. How We Disclose Affiliate Content

3.1 **Clear and conspicuous.** The Publisher aims to disclose affiliate
relationships in a clear and conspicuous manner, placed near the relevant
recommendation and before any Affiliate Link where practicable, consistent with FTC
guidance and applicable platform rules.

3.2 **In-content notices.** Affiliate content may include a short notice such as:
"This post contains affiliate links. If you buy through them, we may earn a
commission at no extra cost to you." Such a notice does not change the meaning of
this Disclosure.

3.3 **Platform tools.** On platforms that provide built-in paid-partnership or
material-connection labels, the Publisher will use those tools where appropriate in

4.1 **Our commitment.** Affiliate Compensation does not control the Publisher's

4.3 **Your responsibility.** You should evaluate any product or service for yourself,
review the merchant's own terms and policies, and make your own purchasing decision.
The Publisher is not a party to your transaction with a merchant.

## 5. Sponsored Content and Other Material Connections

5.1 **Sponsored content.** From time to time the Publisher may publish sponsored
posts, paid placements, or gifted-product reviews. Where a material connection
beyond a standard Affiliate Link exists, the Publisher will identify it (for
example, with a label such as "Sponsored" or "Paid Partnership").

5.2 **Gifts and samples.** If the Publisher receives a free product or sample in
exchange for a review or mention, it will disclose that the item was provided at no
cost.

5.3 **Distinct from affiliate links.** Sponsorship and gifting are different from
affiliate arrangements and may be disclosed separately, but both reflect the
Publisher's commitment to transparency.
