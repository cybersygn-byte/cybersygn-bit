This Apprenticeship Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of
**[EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**") by and between:

**[EMPLOYER / SPONSOR LEGAL NAME]**, a **[STATE] [ENTITY TYPE]** with its principal
place of business at **[EMPLOYER ADDRESS]** (the "**Sponsor**"); and

**[APPRENTICE FULL NAME]**, an individual residing at **[APPRENTICE ADDRESS]** (the
"**Apprentice**").

The Sponsor and the Apprentice are each a "**Party**" and together the
"**Parties**."

**Recitals.** The Sponsor wishes to train the Apprentice in the occupation of
**[TRADE / OCCUPATION]** through a structured program combining on-the-job training
and related instruction, and the Apprentice wishes to learn that occupation. The
Parties intend this Agreement to describe the program, wage progression, and
obligations of each Party. Registered apprenticeship programs may be subject to
additional government standards; the Parties should confirm any applicable program
requirements. In consideration of the mutual promises below, the Parties agree as
follows.

---

## 1. Program and Occupation

1.1 **Occupation.** The Sponsor will train the Apprentice in the occupation of
**[TRADE / OCCUPATION]** in accordance with the program described in this Agreement
and any attached training outline (the "**Program**").

1.2 **Structure.** The Program combines (a) on-the-job training under qualified
supervision and (b) related technical instruction. The Apprentice will complete
approximately **[NUMBER]** hours of on-the-job training and **[NUMBER]** hours of
related instruction.

1.3 **Supervision.** The Apprentice will train under **[MENTOR / JOURNEYWORKER
NAME / TITLE]**, who will provide instruction, oversight, and evaluation.

1.4 **Standards.** **[OPTIONAL: This Program is registered with [APPRENTICESHIP
AGENCY] and follows its standards; the Parties will comply with those standards.]**

## 2. Term and Schedule

2.1 **Term.** The apprenticeship begins on **[START DATE]** and continues for
approximately **[DURATION, e.g. NUMBER years]** or until the Apprentice completes
the required hours and competencies, whichever the Program specifies.

2.2 **Probationary period.** The first **[NUMBER]** days or hours are a
probationary period during which either Party may end the apprenticeship more
readily, consistent with Program standards and applicable law.

2.3 **Schedule.** The Apprentice will work and attend instruction on a schedule set
by the Sponsor, consistent with the Program and applicable law.

## 3. Wages and Wage Progression

3.1 **Starting wage.** The Sponsor will pay the Apprentice a starting wage of
**[$RATE per hour]**, subject to applicable withholdings.

3.2 **Wage progression.** As the Apprentice advances through Program milestones, the
wage will increase according to the schedule in **[SCHEDULE A / the Program]**,
reaching approximately **[PERCENT]%** of the journeyworker wage by completion.

3.3 **Minimum wage compliance.** At all stages, the Apprentice's wage will meet or
exceed the applicable minimum wage and any other legal requirements.

3.4 **Cost of instruction.** **[OPTIONAL: The Sponsor will pay or reimburse the
cost of required related instruction and materials as described in [REFERENCE].]**

## 4. Obligations of the Apprentice

4.1 **Diligence.** The Apprentice will diligently learn the occupation, perform
assigned work competently, attend required instruction, and follow Program
requirements.

4.2 **Conduct and safety.** The Apprentice will comply with the Sponsor's lawful
policies, safety rules, and reasonable instructions, and will report unsafe
conditions.

4.3 **Records.** The Apprentice will keep, and allow the Sponsor to keep, accurate
records of training hours and progress.

## 5. Obligations of the Sponsor

5.1 **Training.** The Sponsor will provide the on-the-job training, supervision, and
access to related instruction needed for the Apprentice to learn the occupation.

5.2 **Safe environment.** The Sponsor will provide a safe working and learning
environment and the tools or equipment the Program requires.

5.3 **Evaluation and advancement.** The Sponsor will evaluate the Apprentice's
progress at reasonable intervals and advance wages and responsibilities as the
Apprentice meets Program milestones.

5.4 **Completion credential.** On successful completion, the Sponsor will provide
the Apprentice with **[A CERTIFICATE OF COMPLETION / CREDENTIAL]** recognizing the
Apprentice's achievement.

## 6. Confidentiality and Property

6.1 **Confidential Information.** The Apprentice will keep the Sponsor's non-public,
proprietary, or trade-secret information confidential during and after the
apprenticeship, except as authorized or required by law.

6.2 **Property and tools.** Sponsor-provided tools, equipment, and materials remain
the Sponsor's property and must be returned on completion or termination, normal
wear excepted.

## 7. Termination

7.1 **By either Party.** Either Party may end the apprenticeship for cause, or as
permitted by the Program standards and applicable law, on written notice describing
the reason.

7.2 **Cancellation and dispute.** Any cancellation or dispute will be handled in
accordance with the Program standards and any applicable agency procedures.

7.3 **Effect.** On termination, the Sponsor will pay all earned but unpaid wages as
required by law, and the Parties will document the hours and competencies completed.

## 8. General Provisions

8.1 **Governing law and venue.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State
of **[STATE]**, without regard to its conflict-of-laws rules. The Parties submit to
the jurisdiction of the courts located in **[COUNTY, STATE]**.

8.2 **Minor consent.** If the Apprentice is under the age of majority, a parent or
legal guardian must co-sign this Agreement, and the Parties will comply with
applicable youth-employment laws.

8.3 **Entire agreement; amendment.** This Agreement, together with the Program
standards it references, is the entire agreement on its subject and supersedes prior
discussions. It may be amended only by a writing signed by both Parties.

8.4 **Severability and waiver.** If any provision is unenforceable, the rest remains
in effect. A Party's failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver.

8.5 **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Agreement may be signed in
counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which is an original and all of
which together form one agreement.
