This Adventure Tour Waiver and Release of Liability (this "**Waiver**") is entered
into as of **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**") by and between:

**[PARTICIPANT NAME]**, residing at **[PARTICIPANT ADDRESS]** (the
"**Participant**"); and

**[TOUR OPERATOR / GUIDE COMPANY LEGAL NAME]**, a **[STATE] [ENTITY TYPE]** with
its principal place of business at **[ADDRESS]** (the "**Operator**").

Participant and Operator are each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**."

**Recitals.** The Operator provides guided adventure and outdoor tours, including
**[DESCRIBE: e.g. white-water rafting, zip-lining, rock climbing, canyoneering,
backcountry hiking, mountain biking, kayaking, off-road or ATV tours, caving,
wildlife tours, snorkeling, or guided expeditions]** (the "**Tour**"). The
Participant wishes to take part in the Tour and understands that adventure
activities take place in remote or natural environments and involve a real risk of
serious injury or death. The Operator permits the Participant to take part only on
the condition that the Participant agrees to this Waiver. In consideration of being
permitted to take part, the Parties agree as follows.

---

## 1. The Tour and Voluntary Participation

1.1 **Description.** The Tour consists of **[DESCRIBE THE ACTIVITIES, ROUTE, AND
DURATION]**, taking place at or around **[LOCATION(S)]** on **[DATE(S)]**, led by
the Operator's guides.

1.2 **Voluntary participation.** The Participant takes part voluntarily and of the
Participant's own free will, may decline any portion of the Tour, and may withdraw
at any time, understanding that withdrawal in a remote area may involve delay,
additional cost, and limited evacuation options.

1.3 **Remote conditions.** The Participant understands that the Tour may occur in
remote locations where weather, terrain, water, and wildlife conditions can change
rapidly, where cellular and emergency services may be limited or unavailable, and
where the response time for rescue or medical care may be long.

1.4 **Guide authority.** The Participant agrees to follow the reasonable
instructions of the guides at all times, including instructions to stop, change
route, or end the Tour for safety, and understands that guides may exclude any
participant whose conduct or condition creates a risk.

## 2. Assumption of Risk

2.1 **Inherent risks.** The Participant understands that adventure tours involve
inherent and significant risks, including **[e.g. drowning and water hazards;
falls from height; rockfall; capsizing; collisions; equipment failure; exposure to
heat, cold, sun, altitude, and weather; difficult or unstable terrain; encounters
with wildlife, insects, and plants; getting lost or separated; vehicle and
watercraft accidents; and the negligence of other participants]**, and that these
risks may result in property damage, minor or serious bodily injury, illness,
permanent disability, or death.

2.2 **Knowing assumption.** Knowing and appreciating these risks, the Participant
freely and voluntarily assumes all risk of loss, damage, injury, illness, or death
arising out of or related to the Tour, including risks arising from conditions of
the natural environment and from the Operator's ordinary negligence to the extent
permitted by law.

2.3 **Conditions beyond control.** The Participant accepts the risk that weather,
water levels, wildlife, and natural conditions are beyond the Operator's control
and may change without warning.

## 3. Participant Responsibilities and Equipment

3.1 **Fitness and skill.** The Participant represents that the Participant has the
physical fitness, swimming ability (where relevant), and experience necessary for
the Tour as described, and has accurately disclosed any limitation.

3.2 **Equipment use.** The Participant will use safety equipment provided by the
Operator (for example, helmets, life jackets, harnesses, or restraints) as
instructed for the entire activity, and will report any equipment that appears
damaged or unsafe.

3.3 **No intoxication.** The Participant will not take part in the Tour while
impaired by alcohol or drugs and understands that the Operator may exclude an
impaired participant without refund.

3.4 **Following directions.** The Participant will stay with the group as
directed, remain within designated areas, and not undertake unsanctioned
activities during the Tour.

## 4. Health Representations and Medical Authorization

4.1 **Fitness to participate.** The Participant represents that the Participant is
in good health and has no medical condition that would make participation unsafe,
or has obtained clearance from a physician to participate.

4.2 **Disclosure.** The Participant has disclosed any condition, injury, allergy,
medication, dietary need, or limitation relevant to safe participation: **[LIST OR
"NONE"]**, and will promptly inform the Operator of any change.

4.3 **Consent to treatment and evacuation.** In the event of injury or a medical
emergency, the Participant authorizes the Operator to arrange first aid, emergency
medical care, evacuation, and transport, and is responsible for the cost of any
treatment, evacuation, and related transport. The Participant is encouraged to
carry travel and evacuation insurance.

4.4 **Emergency contact.** The Participant's emergency contact is **[NAME,
RELATIONSHIP, PHONE]**.

## 5. Release and Waiver of Claims

5.1 **Release.** To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the
Participant releases, waives, and discharges the Operator and its owners,
officers, guides, employees, agents, contractors, and landowners or permittees
whose property is used for the Tour (the "**Released Parties**") from any and all
claims, demands, causes of action, and liability for loss, damage, injury,
illness, or death arising out of or related to the Tour, including claims based on
the ordinary negligence of any Released Party.

5.2 **Covenant not to sue.** The Participant agrees not to sue or bring any claim
against the Released Parties for any matter released under Section 5.1.

5.3 **Limits of the release.** This release does not apply to liability that
applicable law does not permit to be waived, including, in many jurisdictions,
liability for gross negligence, recklessness, or willful or wrongful misconduct.
The scope and enforceability of liability waivers vary by jurisdiction; local law
controls where it limits what may be released.

## 6. Indemnification

6.1 **Indemnity.** To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the
Participant will indemnify and hold harmless the Released Parties from any claim
brought by or on behalf of the Participant, or by a third party, arising out of the
Tour or the Participant's breach of this Waiver, including reasonable attorneys'
fees and the cost of any search, rescue, or evacuation caused by the Participant's
conduct.

6.2 **Exclusions.** The indemnity in Section 6.1 does not extend to liability
arising from a Released Party's gross negligence or willful misconduct, or to the
extent applicable law prohibits indemnification.

## 7. Changes, Media, and Property

7.1 **Itinerary changes.** The Operator may modify, postpone, or cancel all or part
of the Tour for safety, weather, or conditions beyond its control, and any refund
or rescheduling is governed by the Operator's separate booking and cancellation
policy.

7.2 **Media release.** The Participant **[grants / does not grant]** the Operator
permission to capture photographs or video of the Participant during the Tour and
to use that media for the Operator's promotional purposes without further
compensation.

7.3 **Personal property.** The Participant is responsible for the Participant's
personal property, and the Operator is not responsible for lost, stolen, or damaged
property during the Tour.

## 8. Minor (if applicable)

8.1 **Parent or guardian consent.** If the Participant is under the age of majority
in the governing jurisdiction, the undersigned parent or legal guardian represents
that they have legal authority to consent on the Participant's behalf, agrees to
all terms of this Waiver on the Participant's behalf, and, to the fullest extent
permitted by law, releases and indemnifies the Released Parties on the same terms
as the Participant.

8.2 **Acknowledgment.** The parent or guardian understands that, in some
jurisdictions, a parent's pre-injury waiver of a minor's claims may be limited or
unenforceable, and that local law controls.

## 9. General Provisions

9.1 **Governing law and venue.** This Waiver is governed by the laws of the State
of **[STATE]**, without regard to its conflict-of-laws rules. The Parties submit
to the jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in **[COUNTY,
STATE]**.

9.2 **Severability.** If any provision of this Waiver is held unenforceable, the
remaining provisions remain in full force, and the unenforceable provision will be
narrowed or reformed only to the extent needed to make it enforceable, so that the
release operates to the maximum extent the law allows.

9.3 **Entire agreement; amendment.** This Waiver is the entire agreement between
the Parties on its subject and may be amended only by a writing signed by both
Parties. It is intended to supplement, not replace, the Operator's booking terms,
if any.

9.4 **Binding effect.** This Waiver binds and benefits the Parties and their
heirs, next of kin, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.

9.5 **Acknowledgment of understanding.** The Participant has read this Waiver,
understands that it gives up substantial legal rights, including the right to sue,
and signs it freely and voluntarily.

9.6 **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Waiver may be signed in
counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which is an original and all of
which together form one agreement.
