This Travel Consent for a Minor (this "**Consent**") is made as of **[EFFECTIVE
DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**") by:

**[CONSENTING PARENT/GUARDIAN LEGAL NAME]**, residing at **[ADDRESS]** (the
"**Consenting Parent**"); and, where a second parent or guardian signs,

**[SECOND PARENT/GUARDIAN LEGAL NAME]**, residing at **[ADDRESS]** (together with the
Consenting Parent, the "**Consenting Parents**").

This Consent concerns **[MINOR LEGAL NAME]**, a minor born on **[MINOR DATE OF
BIRTH]** (the "**Minor**"), who will travel in the care of **[ACCOMPANYING ADULT
LEGAL NAME]**, residing at **[ADDRESS]** (the "**Accompanying Adult**").

**Recitals.** The Minor will travel without one or both parents or legal guardians
present, and border officials, airlines, schools, and other authorities often
require written, signed evidence that the absent parent or guardian consents to the
travel. The Consenting Parents wish to document that consent and to give the
Accompanying Adult limited authority to act for the Minor during the trip. In
consideration of the matters described below, the Consenting Parents agree and
consent as follows.

---

## 1. Identification of the Minor and the Adults

1.1 **The Minor.** The Minor is **[MINOR LEGAL NAME]**, born **[MINOR DATE OF
BIRTH]**, holder of passport or ID number **[PASSPORT/ID NUMBER]** issued by
**[ISSUING COUNTRY/AUTHORITY]**, expiring **[EXPIRATION DATE]**. The Minor's home
address is **[MINOR ADDRESS]**.

1.2 **Authority of the signer.** Each Consenting Parent represents that he or she is
the parent or court-appointed legal guardian of the Minor and has lawful authority to
consent to the Minor's travel. Any custody order or guardianship affecting that
authority is identified here: **[CASE/ORDER REFERENCE, IF ANY]**.

1.3 **The Accompanying Adult.** The Accompanying Adult is **[ACCOMPANYING ADULT LEGAL
NAME]**, born **[DATE OF BIRTH]**, holder of passport or ID number **[PASSPORT/ID
NUMBER]**, whose relationship to the Minor is **[RELATIONSHIP]**.

## 2. Travel Details

2.1 **Itinerary.** The Minor is authorized to travel from **[ORIGIN]** to
**[DESTINATION(S)]**, departing on or about **[DEPARTURE DATE]** and returning on or
about **[RETURN DATE]**.

2.2 **Means of travel.** The Minor will travel by **[AIR / CAR / RAIL / SEA / OTHER]**,
on carrier and flight or trip reference **[CARRIER / FLIGHT / CONFIRMATION]**, with
expected accommodations at **[LODGING/CONTACT WHILE TRAVELING]**.

2.3 **Purpose.** The purpose of the trip is **[PURPOSE, e.g. vacation, visiting
family, school program, sporting event]**.

2.4 **Changes.** Minor changes to schedule, routing, or carrier within the same
destination region are within the scope of this Consent. A change of destination
country or a materially longer trip requires a new or amended Consent.

## 3. Scope of Consent and Delegated Authority

3.1 **Consent to travel.** The Consenting Parents consent to the Minor's travel as
described in Section 2 and authorize relevant authorities to permit the Minor to
depart, transit, and return.

3.2 **Day-to-day authority.** The Consenting Parents authorize the Accompanying Adult
to make ordinary day-to-day decisions for the Minor during the trip, including lodging,
meals, local transportation, supervision, and routine activities.

3.3 **Medical authority.** The Consenting Parents authorize the Accompanying Adult to
consent to medical, dental, and emergency care for the Minor while traveling, as more
fully addressed in Section 5.

3.4 **Limits.** The Accompanying Adult may **[NOT]** do the following without first
attempting to reach a Consenting Parent: **[LIST ANY LIMITS, OR "None"]**. No limit
prevents action a provider or authority deems immediately necessary for the Minor's
safety.

## 4. Consent of the Non-Traveling Parent

4.1 **Confirmation.** Each Consenting Parent who is not traveling with the Minor
confirms by signing below that he or she consents to the Minor leaving the place of
residence with the Accompanying Adult and traveling as described.

4.2 **Sole authority.** If only one parent or guardian holds lawful authority to
consent (for example, by reason of sole custody, death, or a court order), that fact
and the supporting documentation are described here: **[EXPLANATION AND DOCUMENT
REFERENCE, IF APPLICABLE]**.

4.3 **No interference.** Nothing in this Consent waives or alters any custody or
parenting arrangement; it documents consent for this specific trip only.

## 5. Medical Care While Traveling

5.1 **Authorization.** The Consenting Parents authorize the Accompanying Adult to
consent to examination, diagnosis, and treatment of the Minor by licensed providers
during the trip, including emergency care.

5.2 **Medical background.** The Minor's known conditions and allergies are **[LIST OR
"NONE KNOWN"]**, current medications are **[LIST OR "NONE"]**, and blood type, if
relevant, is **[BLOOD TYPE]**.

5.3 **Insurance.** The Minor's health or travel insurance is carrier **[CARRIER]**,
policy number **[POLICY NUMBER]**, with assistance line **[PHONE]**. The Consenting
Parents remain financially responsible for the Minor's care.

## 6. Emergency Contacts

6.1 **Consenting Parents.** Reach a Consenting Parent at phone **[PHONE]**, alternate
**[ALTERNATE PHONE]**, email **[EMAIL]**.

6.2 **Local contact.** A contact at or near the destination is **[NAME, RELATIONSHIP,
PHONE]**.

6.3 **No delay.** Inability to reach a listed contact does not prevent the Accompanying
Adult from acting, or an authority from assisting, where the Minor's safety requires
prompt action.

## 7. Acknowledgments and Indemnification

7.1 **Good-faith reliance.** The Consenting Parents acknowledge that border officials,
carriers, providers, and the Accompanying Adult will rely on this Consent in good faith
and agree that such reliance is reasonable.

7.2 **Hold harmless.** To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the
Consenting Parents will not hold the Accompanying Adult liable for good-faith decisions
made under this Consent, except for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

7.3 **Accuracy.** The Consenting Parents represent that the information in this Consent
is accurate as of the Effective Date.

## 8. Revocation, Governing Law, and General Provisions

8.1 **Revocation.** A Consenting Parent may revoke this Consent before departure by
written notice to the Accompanying Adult. Revocation does not affect actions already
taken in reliance on the Consent or steps underway in an emergency.

8.2 **Governing law.** This Consent is governed by the laws of the State of **[STATE]**.
The entry, exit, and documentation rules of each destination and transit country, and
of the Minor's home country, govern where they differ from this document, and the
Consenting Parents should confirm those rules before travel.

8.3 **Witness or notarization.** Many authorities require this Consent to be
notarized, and some require apostille or consular legalization. The Consenting Parents
will complete the notarization block below where required.

8.4 **Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions stay
in effect.

8.5 **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Consent may be signed in
counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which is an original, except where an
authority requires a wet-ink or notarized original.
