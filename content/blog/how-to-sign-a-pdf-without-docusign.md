The most-asked question I get from new CyberSygn users is some variation of: "wait, you don't need DocuSign for this?"

You really don't. Here are the actual ways to sign a PDF in 2026, ranked by how much they respect your time.

## The four options

### 1. CyberSygn (yes, this is a sales post, but stay with me)

Drop a PDF, watch every signature line, initial, date, and checkbox appear automatically. Send it to anyone with a magic link. They sign in their browser — no account, no app. The signed PDF comes back to you with a SHA-256 audit certificate.

Free for three documents lifetime. Origin tier is $9/month locked for life if you're one of the first hundred founders.

### 2. Adobe Acrobat Reader (free, but slow)

Open the PDF, Tools → Fill & Sign → Sign Yourself → draw or type your signature, drag it onto the page, save. Works fine for one-off self-signing. The friction shows up when you need someone else to sign — Adobe pushes you toward Adobe Sign at $24.99/month.

Best for: a contract you need to sign once for yourself.

### 3. Preview on macOS (free, surprisingly capable)

Mac users: Open the PDF in Preview, click the markup icon, click the signature icon, choose "Create signature" and either trackpad-sign or hold a piece of paper up to your camera. Place the signature, save.

Best for: Mac users who only need to self-sign and don't need an audit trail.

### 4. DocuSign or Dropbox Sign (slow and expensive)

The incumbents. Both make you upload, drag every field by hand, configure routing, send via email, and the receiver has to create an account in many flows. They charge $25-30/month for Personal tiers. Same legal weight as the other three options, ten times the friction.

Best for: enterprise legal teams who need integrations the others don't offer.

## What "ESIGN-compliant" actually means

Every option above produces a legally binding signature under the U.S. ESIGN Act and UETA in most states. The legal framework was deliberately written to be tool-agnostic — what matters is the intent to sign and the audit trail, not which company captured the signature.

If anyone tells you "you have to use DocuSign because of compliance," they're either misinformed or selling you something. Audit certificates from CyberSygn (and Adobe Sign, and DocuSign) all satisfy the same legal standards.

## The decision tree

- **Signing a contract for yourself, once?** Preview (Mac) or Acrobat (free).
- **Sending a contract to one or two other people, occasionally?** CyberSygn free tier.
- **Sending contracts regularly?** CyberSygn Origin or Solo. The field-detection saves about 30 minutes per contract.
- **Need enterprise integrations (Salesforce, etc.)?** DocuSign Business. CyberSygn isn't there yet.
