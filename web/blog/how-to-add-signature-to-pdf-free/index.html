How to add a signature to a contract for free

Step-by-step on adding a signature to a PDF without paying for DocuSign, with multiple free options ranked by speed and use case.

If you just need to sign a PDF — once, today, for free — here are the four fastest paths. No subscription required.

Path 1: Use CyberSygn's free tier (recommended)

  1. Go to cybersygn.io/preview/.
  2. Drag a PDF onto the page, or click to select one.
  3. Watch the signature line appear automatically — usually about three seconds.
  4. Click the signature box and either type your name, upload a PNG of your signature, or draw with your mouse.
  5. Click Download PDF. The signed file goes straight to your downloads folder.

Time: about 90 seconds.

This works for any PDF, even ones with multiple signature lines, initials, dates, or checkboxes. The free tier gives you three documents lifetime — enough to evaluate seriously.

Path 2: macOS Preview (Mac only)

If you're on a Mac and just need to self-sign one document:

  1. Open the PDF in Preview.
  2. Click the Markup icon (looks like a pencil tip in a circle, near the top right).
  3. Click the Signature icon. The first time, choose Create signature.
  4. Use your trackpad to draw your signature, OR sign a piece of paper and hold it up to your camera. (The camera option captures a clean image.)
  5. Place the signature on the page by clicking the saved signature in the dropdown.
  6. File → Save. Done.

Time: about 2 minutes the first time (to capture your signature), 30 seconds every time after.

Preview can't send documents to other people for signing, and it doesn't produce an audit certificate. For self-signing only.

Path 3: Adobe Acrobat Reader (any platform)

  1. Download Adobe Acrobat Reader for free.
  2. Open the PDF.
  3. Tools → Fill & Sign → Sign Yourself → Add Signature.
  4. Type, draw, or upload your signature.
  5. Drag the signature onto the document.
  6. File → Save. Done.

Time: about 2 minutes.

Acrobat Reader is a heavy install (about 350 MB) for what it does. Fine if you already have it; not worth installing just for signing.

Path 4: SmallPDF, ILovePDF, PDFescape (browser-based)

Various free web tools let you upload a PDF, click to place a signature, type or draw, and download.

The trade-off: you're uploading your contract to someone else's server. For sensitive documents, this is a real privacy concern. CyberSygn runs the detection in YOUR browser; the PDF doesn't leave your machine unless you choose to send it.

Free tier limits on these tools are usually 2-3 documents per day.

What about signing on phone?

All four options above work on mobile. CyberSygn is the fastest because the field detection means you don't have to zoom in to drag boxes around a small screen.

The macOS Preview equivalent on iPhone is to open the PDF in Files → Markup → + and choose Signature. Works for self-signing only.
