Download Signed PDF and Audit Certificate: The Two Files You Get

Two files land for every signed document: the PDF for the parties, and the certificate for proof.

Most people assume a signed contract is a single file, but with CyberSygn it is always two. Every time you download signed PDF files here, a SHA-256 audit certificate comes alongside them automatically. The PDF is the contract everyone agreed to, while the certificate is the evidence pack that proves nobody touched the document after signing. Both are ready the second the last signer finishes, and both stay stored for as long as your account remains active. In this guide you will learn exactly where to find both files, how every signer receives their copy without any work from you, and how to keep a clean long-term archive so you are never scrambling for proof later. By the end, treating these as a matched pair will feel like second nature.

Where to find and download signed PDF files

Start in your dashboard, where clicking any completed document brings you to the document detail view that serves as your control center for that contract. There you will see a preview of the signed PDF, a preview of the audit certificate, and a dedicated download button for each, so a signed contract download is always one click away. If you want both at once, the combined download button hands you the two files as a single ZIP, which is the fastest way to grab everything together. The same screen gives you an audit certificate download on its own, in case you only need the proof file for a particular request. So when you go to download signed PDF files, you are never hunting through scattered folders, because everything for that contract sits together on one screen. The SHA-256 audit certificate is the file that makes the contract defensible, since SHA-256 is essentially a digital fingerprint of the document. If even one character of the signed PDF changes, the fingerprint no longer matches, and any tampering shows up instantly. That is why the certificate is not paperwork but evidence. One more detail is worth knowing: these downloads are mobile-friendly. Whether you are on a phone, a tablet, or a laptop, the buttons behave the same way and the files arrive in the same clean pair. You do not even have to visit the dashboard to get them, because both files are attached to the completion email automatically. That gives you two paths to the same documents, so you can take whichever is faster in the moment.

How counterparties get their copy automatically

Here is a step you can stop doing by hand entirely: forwarding the signed contract to everyone who needs it. CyberSygn does it for you the moment a document completes, when every signer automatically receives the signed PDF and the audit certificate by email. You forward nothing, you chase no one for a copy, and you, as the sender, receive the exact same pair of files. So everyone who signed walks away with both the contract and the proof sitting in their own inbox, without a single manual step from you. But what about someone who needs the document yet was never part of the signing flow, such as a new bookkeeper, a partner who joined the project late, or an accountant who handles your invoicing? That case is easy too, because you simply open the dashboard, download the signed PDF and certificate fresh, and attach them to your own email. The dashboard copy is always current and always available, so you can hand off the signed contract to anyone at any time without re-running the signing process. This matters more than it first sounds, because manual forwarding is exactly where small businesses lose documents, attach the wrong version, or forget someone entirely. Automatic delivery plus an always-ready dashboard copy means the right file reaches the right person every time.

Keeping a clean long-term archive

CyberSygn stores your signed documents and audit certificates indefinitely on paid plans, which means you can return in a year, or even three years later, and download fresh copies whenever you need them. The platform functions as your filing cabinet, and it does not lose files. Even so, careful operators keep their own archive as well, and there is a simple rule for doing it right: whenever you export signed document files, pull the signed PDF and the certificate together, because they belong as a pair. The PDF without the certificate is just a document, and the certificate without the PDF is just a fingerprint, but together they prove the contract is genuine and untouched. For one extra layer of safety, some operators also save a separate SHA-256 hash of the certificate itself, which gives them a check against any future challenge to the certificate rather than only the contract. It is a small habit that pays off if anyone ever questions the record. Here is a system that works reliably: make one folder per client, and inside it save each signed PDF and its certificate as a matched pair, named with the contract title and the date. Then, if a question ever arises about whether a contract is real or whether someone altered it, you reach for the pair and the answer is right there waiting. Store it once, label it clearly, and your evidence is ready the day you need it rather than the day after. Every time you download signed PDF files and save the matched certificate beside them, you are building a record that holds up under any challenge.
